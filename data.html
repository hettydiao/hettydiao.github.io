Data I Worked With

I have extensive experience working with various types of large data, including large survey, cancer registry, clinical trials, and electronic health record data:

  • Health and Retirement Study (HRS) – A long-term panel study surveying older Americans to gather data on health, retirement, income, and wealth.
  • Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID) – A longitudinal panel survey of U.S. individuals and families to assess economic, social, and health factors over time.
  • Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS) – A set of large-scale surveys of families and individuals, their medical providers, and employers to collect data on the cost and use of healthcare.
  • National Health Interview Survey (NHIS) – A national survey used to track the health of the U.S. population by collecting data on a broad range of health topics.
  • Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results (SEER) – A large cancer registry program that provides information on cancer statistics and trends across the U.S.
  • National Lung Screening Trial (NLST) – A large clinical trial aimed at determining if low-dose CT scans are effective in reducing lung cancer deaths compared to chest x-rays.

Through working with these datasets, I have developed expertise in cleaning, managing, and analyzing large datasets, making informed decisions using econometric and statistical tools.
